# F9-UC6 Critical Humidex Thresholds

## Objective

Identify humidex thresholds that are relevant for comfort and heat-risk analysis.

Humidex combines temperature and humidity into a perceived heat index. It is useful for safety context but should not be treated as the complete definition of livability.

## Threshold Scale

The current project uses this operational scale from the provided research material:

| Humidex range | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Below 30 | Little or no discomfort |
| 30 to 39 | Some discomfort |
| 40 to 45 | Great discomfort; reduce exertion |
| Above 45 | Dangerous |
| Above 54 | Imminent heat-stroke risk in occupational guidance |

## How Humidex Should Be Used

For F9:

- as an input feature,
- as a safety context variable,
- as an ablation variable to test whether the model relies too strongly on it.

For F10:

- as part of rule-based risk labels,
- as a critical alert threshold,
- as a safety constraint for future recommendations.

## Important Limitation

Humidex does not include clothing, activity level, radiant heat, adaptation, or personal preference. It should not replace a full comfort model.
